IR Activity Selection Rule

A mode is IR active only if it produces a change in dipole moment:

$$\left(\frac{\partial \mu}{\partial Q}\right)_0 \neq 0$$

Symmetric modes of centrosymmetric molecules (e.g. CO₂ symmetric stretch) are IR inactive.

Vibrational Energy Levels

In the harmonic oscillator approximation, levels are equally spaced:

$$E_v = h\tilde{\nu}\left(v + \frac{1}{2}\right), \quad v = 0, 1, 2, \ldots$$

The fundamental IR absorption is $v = 0 \to 1$ at wavenumber $\tilde{\nu}$.

Number of Normal Modes

$$\text{Linear: } 3N - 5 \quad\quad \text{Nonlinear: } 3N - 6$$

e.g. CO₂ (linear, N=3): 4 modes. H₂O (nonlinear, N=3): 3 modes.
