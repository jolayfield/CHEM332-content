sp — Linear (180°)

s character: 50%  |  Examples: C≡C, CO₂, HCN

$$|\psi_1\rangle=\tfrac{1}{\sqrt{2}}|s\rangle+\tfrac{1}{\sqrt{2}}|p_z\rangle$$ $$|\psi_2\rangle=\tfrac{1}{\sqrt{2}}|s\rangle-\tfrac{1}{\sqrt{2}}|p_z\rangle$$

Two unhybridised $p$ orbitals remain and form two $\pi$ bonds in triple bonds.

sp² — Trigonal Planar (120°)

s character: 33%  |  Examples: C=C, BF₃, benzene

$$|\psi_1\rangle=\tfrac{1}{\sqrt{3}}|s\rangle+\sqrt{\tfrac{2}{3}}|p_z\rangle$$ $$|\psi_2\rangle=\tfrac{1}{\sqrt{3}}|s\rangle-\tfrac{1}{\sqrt{6}}|p_z\rangle+\tfrac{1}{\sqrt{2}}|p_x\rangle$$

One unhybridised $p$ orbital remains perpendicular to the plane, forming a $\pi$ bond.

sp³ — Tetrahedral (109.47°)

s character: 25%  |  Examples: CH₄, NH₃, H₂O

$$|\psi_1\rangle=\tfrac{1}{2}(|s\rangle+|p_x\rangle+|p_y\rangle+|p_z\rangle)$$ $$|\psi_2\rangle=\tfrac{1}{2}(|s\rangle+|p_x\rangle-|p_y\rangle-|p_z\rangle)$$
  • No unhybridised $p$ orbitals remain
  • Higher $s$ character → shorter, stronger bonds
  • Lone pairs reduce angle below 109.47°

Mathematical Foundation: LCAO

Every hybrid orbital is a normalised linear combination of atomic orbitals:

$$|\psi_h\rangle = c_s|s\rangle + c_{p_x}|p_x\rangle + c_{p_y}|p_y\rangle + c_{p_z}|p_z\rangle \qquad \text{subject to} \qquad c_s^2 + c_{p_x}^2 + c_{p_y}^2 + c_{p_z}^2 = 1$$

The squared coefficient $c_s^2$ gives the s character, which governs bond angles, electronegativity, and acid strength. Higher $s$ character means the electrons are held closer to the nucleus, resulting in shorter and stronger bonds.
